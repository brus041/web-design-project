We can see right away that most locations have fairly low windspeeds as that is where most of the data points are in this plot. However, even though the data points are sparse, we can see that windspeed picks up at both ends of the x-axis, or at the north and south poles. Arctic winds are a common occurance in those locations, with the weather being slightly more unstable there as well. As a result of these observations one could model this with polynomial regression with degree equal to two, as the above trends describe a parabolic behavior.
